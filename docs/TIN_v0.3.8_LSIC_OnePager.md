### Executive Summary
TIN demonstrates the first fully open-source, physics-integrated simulation of a hybrid polar smallsat + ELFO tolerant network capable of delivering **critical emergency communication in under 3.4 minutes worst-case** from the lunar South Pole.

With only 8 inexpensive 400 km polar relays + the already-funded Lunar Pathfinder ELFO acting as the intelligent DTN/AI hub, TIN achieves **100 % South Pole coverage** and **100 % bundle delivery** over a full 28-day lunar cycle.

### Comparison Context
Compared to emerging commercial lunar relay concepts (e.g., Intuitive Machines LDN or a hypothetical Starlink Lunar-style constellation), TIN is purpose-built for the South Pole with a lean, low-cost architecture. It delivers faster worst-case emergency communication than current commercial plans while remaining fully open-source and immediately reproducible. A broader Starlink Lunar approach would excel at high-bandwidth global service but at significantly higher cost and complexity; TIN focuses on reliable, tolerant connectivity exactly where Artemis needs it most today.
